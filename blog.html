Recent work and professional highlights

Currently working as a Machine Learning Engineer at Suno AI, where I focus on developing AI-powered music generation technologies. My work involves natural language processing, machine learning model development, and building scalable systems for creative AI applications.

Previously, I completed my Ph.D. in Physics at Harvard University, where my research focused on particle physics experiments at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) with the ATLAS collaboration. My thesis work involved searching for di-Higgs boson production, which remains one of the most challenging measurements in particle physics.

After completing my Ph.D., I transitioned from physics research to machine learning and data science, applying the analytical and problem-solving skills I developed in experimental physics to AI and software engineering challenges.
